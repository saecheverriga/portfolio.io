Cyclistic bike-share

analysis case study

Santiago Echeverri
  • Cyclistic is a bike-share company in Chicago that offers a fun, convenient, and affordable way to get around the city. Founded in 2016, Cyclistic has a fleet of 5,824 bikes and 692 stations. Riders can unlock bikes from any station and return them to any other station in the system. Cyclistic is open 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, and its team of dedicated employees are committed to providing a great biking experience for all users. In this case study, I show a data analysis for a fictional bike-share company in order to help them attract more riders.

Ask

The business task for the company is to understand how casual riders and annual members use Cyclistic bikes differently, in order to design a new marketing strategy to convert casual riders into annual members.

Prepare

Source of data

For the purposes of this case study,the datasets has been made available by Motivate International Inc. under license.

How's data organized

This dataset contains information about rides, such as bike type (docked, classic and electric), start and end stations names and ID's with geographical lat and long coordenates and last the rideable type (casual or member).

Sorts and filters made

In this case I used the data from 1 year, starting april 2022 to may 2023, taking from the total of 5.83 million rows, a sample of 200000 which represents more than 99.999% in confidence level and almost 0.5% in margin of error. It was made by using RStudio using merge and sample functions. (see process steps)

Process

Using RStudio

  • With function transform, mutate and wday I extracted data about day of the month and week, month and year.
  • I used the library geosphere for calulating the distance among coordenates, i used functions such as distHaversine and as.isnumeric and also I used vectors for recheck data calculations.
  • For checking the process steps, i used the glimpse funtion. I also used the cbind function for checking purposes. At the end I extracted the information as a .csv file.

Using Spreadsheets

  • I used functions such as left, iferror, extract and joins to fix long decimals data.
  • I checked for irregular values with fixed formulas.
