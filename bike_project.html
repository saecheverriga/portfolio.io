Cyclistic bike-share

analysis study case

Santiago Echeverri
  • Cyclistic is a bike-share company in Chicago that offers a fun, convenient, and affordable way to get around the city. Founded in 2016, Cyclistic has a fleet of 5,824 bikes and 692 stations. Riders can unlock bikes from any station and return them to any other station in the system. Cyclistic is open 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, and its team of dedicated employees are committed to providing a great biking experience for all users. In this case study, I show a data analysis for a fictional bike-share company in order to help them attract more riders.

Ask

The business task for the company is to understand how casual riders and annual members use Cyclistic bikes differently, in order to design a new marketing strategy to convert casual riders into annual members.

Prepare

Source of data

For the purposes of this case study,the datasets has been made available by Motivate International Inc. under license.

How's data organized

This dataset contains information about rides, such as bike type (docked, classic and electric), start and end stations names and ID's with geographical lat and long coordenates and last the rideable type (casual or member).

Sorts and filters made

In this case I used the data from 1 year, starting april 2022 to may 2023, taking from the total of 5.83 million rows, a sample of 200000 which represents more than 99.999% in confidence level and almost 0.5% in margin of error. It was made by using RStudio using merge and sample functions. (see process steps)

Process

Using RStudio

  • With function transform, mutate and wday I extracted data about day of the month and week, month and year.
  • I used the library geosphere for calulating the distance among coordenates, i used functions such as distHaversine and as.isnumeric and also I used vectors for recheck data calculations.
  • For checking the process steps, i used the glimpse funtion. I also used the cbind function for checking purposes. At the end I extracted the information as a .csv file.

Using Spreadsheets

  • I used functions such as left, iferror, extract and joins to fix long decimals data.
  • I checked for irregular values with fixed formulas.

Analyze

  • The data shows that both types of users frequent the stations closest to the beach, complementarily, only a few use the system in the areas far from the beach for both users.
  • The data reveals that the classic bicycle is the most used by both types of users, with member users using it more compared to casual users. The peak usage is observed in the months of June, July, and August for both types of users.
  • Data shows that casual users use the bicycles on weekends, with Saturday and Sunday being their peak days. On the other hand, member users use them during weekdays, with Wednesday and Thursday being their peak days.
  • Casual members take more average time on their rides and cover more average distance. On the other hand, members take less average travel time and cover less average distance.
  • It can also be analyzed that the average speed of member cyclists is higher than the average speed of casual cyclists. Additionally, it is analized that the months with the highest average distance covered for both cyclists are June and July, while October and April have the lowest average distance covered for both cyclists.

Share

Act

  • Membership Benefits and Incentives: Offer exclusive benefits and incentives to casual users who sign up for a membership. This could include discounted rates, priority access to bikes during peak hours, free additional ride time, or access to special events or promotions. Highlight the advantages of being a member, such as cost savings, convenience, and a more personalized experience.
  • Focus on peak months: Since June, July, and August are the peak months for both types of users, it would be wise to allocate additional resources during these periods. This could involve ensuring a higher number of available bikes, increasing staff presence at stations, and implementing efficient maintenance schedules to meet the increased demand.
  • Improve accessibility in areas distant from the beach: Since the data shows that there are fewer users in these areas, it would be beneficial to enhance the bike-sharing system's presence and infrastructure in these regions. This could involve increasing the number of stations and bikes available, as well as promoting the system's benefits to attract more users.
